Chest Exercises

Barbell Bench Press

Description: The barbell bench press is a classic chest exercise that targets the pectoral muscles. It is performed by lying on a bench and pressing a barbell upwards until the arms are fully extended.Do it as 4 reps with 15 counts.

Dumbbell Flyes

Description: Dumbbell flyes are another effective chest exercise that isolates the pectoral muscles. It involves lying on a bench and extending the arms out to the sides with dumbbells, then bringing them back together in a controlled manner.

Incline Bench Press

Description: The incline bench press is a variation of the bench press that targets the upper portion of the chest. It is performed on an inclined bench with a barbell, similar to the standard bench press.

Cable Crossover

Description: Cable crossovers are a great chest exercise that provide constant tension on the pectoral muscles throughout the movement. They are performed using a cable machine with handles attached at shoulder height.

Dumbbell Bench Press

Description: The dumbbell bench press is a variation of the barbell bench press that allows for greater range of motion and stabilization. It is performed using dumbbells instead of a barbell.

Push-ups

Description: Push-ups are a bodyweight exercise that effectively target the chest, shoulders, and triceps. They are performed by lowering the body to the ground and pushing back up using the arms.
